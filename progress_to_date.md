- **副詞** - adverb
- **助詞** - particle

### Additional Parts of Speech

- **助動詞** - auxiliary verb
- **連体詞** - non-conjugating noun modifier (pre-noun adjective)
- **感動詞** - interjection
- **接続詞** - conjunction
- **指示語** - demonstrative words

### Special Categories

- **形式名詞** - formal noun / nominalizer

## 6. Grammar Structures

### Temporal Relations

- **〜間に** - between/during/while
  - Physical space between: "XとYの間に"
  - Temporal during: "action + 間に"
  - While action is happening: "verb + ている + 間に"

### Logical Expectation

- **〜はずだ** - should/ought to (logical expectation)
  - Structure: [clause in plain form] + はずだ
  - Expresses strong expectation based on reasoning
  - Negative: 〜はずがない (there's no way that...)
  - Past expectation: 〜はずだった (was supposed to be)

### State vs. Action (〜ている)

- **ている for resulting state**: With change-of-state verbs
  - 閉まっている = is closed (state), not closing
  - 開いている = is open (state)
  - 壊れている = is broken (state)
  - 結婚している = is married (state)
  - 消えている = is off/gone (state)
- **ている for ongoing action**: With action verbs
  - 食べている = is eating
  - 話している = is speaking

### Aspect Markers

- **〜かける** - to start/begin to do something; almost did something
  - 閉めかける = start to close
  - 食べかける = start to eat
  - Used to mark incomplete or beginning actions

### Verb Constructions

- **話しかける** - to speak to someone (initiate conversation)
  - Person + に + 話しかける

## 7. Analysis Terms

- **活用形** - conjugation pattern
- **文法事項** - grammar point
- **例文** - example sentence
- **結果状態** - resulting state

## 8. Grammatical Patterns

### Making Decisions/Intentions

- **〜ことにする** - decide to do something
- **〜ことになる** - it has been decided / turns out that
- **〜ようと思う** - think/intend to do something

### Habitual Actions

- **〜ようにしている** - make sure to / make it a habit to

### Obligation

- **〜なければならない** - must do something

### Purpose

- **〜ために** - for the purpose of / in order to
- **〜ように** - in order to / so that

### Conditionals

- **〜ば** - if/when (conditional)
- **〜たら** - if/when (conditional - completed action)
- **〜なら** - if (conditional - context-based)
- **〜と** - when/if (natural consequence)

### Causative Forms

- **〜せる** - causative (for u-verbs)
- **〜させる** - causative (for ru-verbs)
- Make/let someone do something

### Passive Forms

- **〜れる** - passive (for u-verbs)
- **〜られる** - passive (for ru-verbs)
- Receive action (sometimes negative meaning)

### Causative-Passive

- **させられる** - causative-passive (ru-verbs)
- **される** - causative-passive (some u-verbs)
- Forced to do something by someone else

### Giving and Receiving

- **あげる** - to give (to someone)
- **くれる** - to give (to me/someone close)
- **もらう** - to receive/get
- Direction of giving matters in Japanese

### Potential Forms

- **〜れる** - potential (u-verbs)
- **〜られる** - potential (ru-verbs)
- Can/be able to do something

### Imperative Forms

- **なさい** - please do/more polite command
- **ろ/よ** - command form (masculine)
- **〜な** - negative command (don't!)

### Volitional Forms

- **〜よう** - let's / shall I (ru-verbs)
- **〜おう** - let's / shall I (u-verbs)
- Intentions or suggestions

### Attempt/Try

- **〜てみる** - try doing something
- Give something a try

### Experience

- **〜たことがある** - have done something before
- Past experience

### Completion/Regret

- **〜てしまう** - complete something / regrettable completion
- Often shortened to 〜ちゃう / 〜じまう

### Ongoing State

- **〜つつある** - in the process of (formal)
- Gradual change in progress

### Appearance/Hearsay

- **〜そうだ** - looks like / I heard
- Conjecture based on appearance (い-adjective stem + そうだ)
- Conjecture based on hearsay (verb plain form + そうだ)
- **〜らしい** - apparently / it seems (hearsay/evidence)
- **〜みたいだ** - looks like / similar to (informal appearance)
- **〜ようだ** - seems like / as if (formal appearance)

### Quotation

- **〜という** - quoting someone
- **〜って** - casual quotation (spoken)

### Reason/Cause

- **〜から** - because (subjective reason)
- **〜ので** - because (polite/ objective reason)
- **〜て** - because (te-form cause)

### Simultaneous Action

- **〜ながら** - while doing (simultaneous action)
- **〜たり〜たり** - doing things like X and Y (example actions)

### Prohibition

- **〜てはいけない** - must not do something
- Prohibited actions

### Lack of Necessity

- **〜なくてもいい** - don't have to do / it's okay not to do
- Optional actions

### Excess

- **〜すぎる** - too much / excessive
- Doing something too much

### Ease/Difficulty

- **〜やすい** - easy to do
- **〜にくい** - hard to do

### Timing

- **〜ところだ** - just did / was doing / about to do
- 辞書形 + ところ = about to do
- ている + ところ = in the middle of doing
- た + ところ = just did

### Change of Direction/Time

- **〜てくる** - toward now (change reaching present) / come and do
- **〜ていく** - away from now (change continuing forward) / go and do
- Process vs. direction distinction

### Partial Negation

- **〜わけではない** - it's not that / it doesn't mean that
- Denies extreme interpretation, not the whole situation
- "not necessarily X"

### State Persistence

- **〜まま** - in that state / as it is / without changing
- State remains unchanged from before

### Depth/Completion

- **〜込む** - do something deeply/completely/thoroughly
- 座り込む (sink down and sit), 考え込む (think deeply), etc.

### Gradual Change

- **〜ようになる** - come to be able to / gradually becomes
- Change in ability, habit, or state over time

### Attempt/Verge

- **〜ようとする** - attempt to / about to try / on the verge of
- Effort or attempt before action

### Without Doing

- **〜ずに** - without doing (formal/literary)
- More formal version of 〜ないで

### Experience/Occasional

- **〜ことがある** - sometimes / there are times when
- Also: たことがある (have done before)

### Preparation

- **〜ておく** - do in advance / do and leave in that state
- Preparation for future use

### Nominalization

- **〜こと** - nominalizer (abstract/the act of)
- **〜の** - nominalizer (concrete/factual)

### Cleft Sentences

- **〜のは〜だ** - emphasis structure
- What I want to say is...

# Additional Progress Since Previous Version

## Aspect and Verb-Class Interpretation

### 継続動詞 vs 瞬間動詞

- **継続動詞** - durative/action verbs
  - 食べる
  - 話す
  - 歩く

- **瞬間動詞** - instantaneous/change-of-state verbs
  - 死ぬ
  - 座る
  - 分かる
  - 結婚する

### Important Aspect Insight

- 〜ている does NOT simply mean English “-ing”
- Interpretation depends on verb type and アスペクト

Examples:

- 食べている = is eating (ongoing action)
- 死んでいる = is dead (resulting state)
- 分かっている = it is clear / understood
- 座っている = is sitting

---

## 分かる as State Change

### Structural Interpretation

- 分かる is structurally closer to:
  - “become clear”
  - “come into clarity”

### State Forms

- 分かった
  - understanding occurred
  - “I got it”

- 分かっている
  - state of clarity exists
  - “I understand”

### Important Distinction

Correct replies to:
- 分かりましたか？

Correct:
- はい、分かりました。
- はい、分かっています。

Incorrect:
- はい、分かります。

Reason:
- 分かります is non-past/general rather than completed or resulting-state aspect.

- stop doing

## Expanded する Structural System

### Core Insight

する is one abstract verb interpreted through constructions.

Core semantic idea:
- manifest
- realise
- bring about

---

### ① 行為のする

Structure:
- N を する

Meaning:
- perform action

Examples:
- 勉強をする
- 運動をする

---

### ② 状態操作のする

Structure:
- N に する

Meaning:
- set state
- choose
- make into

Examples:
- 静かにする
- これにする

---

### ③ 現象のする

Structure:
- N が する

Meaning:
- phenomenon occurs

Examples:
- 音がする
- においがする
- 感じがする
- 気がする

---

### ④ 状態描写のする

Structure:
- N をしている

Meaning:
- be in state characterised by X

Examples:
- 変な顔をしている
- 困った顔をしている
- 大きな目をしている

Important:
- not necessarily action
- not simple possession
- state manifestation

- 毎日運動するようにしている

### 〜ように

Meaning:
- so that
- in order that

Examples:
- 分かるように話す

### 〜ようだ

Meaning:
- seems like
- resembles

Examples:

## 信じる Nuance

信じる may mean:
- believe
- trust
- have confidence in

Context determines nuance.

Examples:

- 彼を信じる
  - trust him

- そのゲームを信じる
  - trust the game
  - have confidence in it

## まったく Polarity Sensitivity

### With Negatives

Meaning:
- not at all

Examples:
- まったく分からない
- まったく悲しい感じがしない

---

### With Positives

Meaning:
- really
- absolutely
- emotionally emphatic

Examples:
- まったく困った人だ
- まったくその通りだ

## Conditional Interpretation

### 〜と

Important insight:

- often interpreted as natural consequence
- lower uncertainty than English “if”

Examples:

- ドアを開けると、音がした。
- 入ると、先生がいた。

Often closer to:
- “when”
than:
- “if”

---

## Adverbial ナ形容詞 Usage

### ナ形容詞 + に + 動詞

Meaning:
- adverbial manner

Examples:
- 静かに話す
- 静かに歩く

Important distinction:

- 静かにする
  - make quiet

vs

- 静かに話す
  - speak quietly

# Structural Insights Learned

## Japanese Often Expresses State Transitions

Examples:

- 静かにしなくなった
- 信じるようになった

rather than directly expressing opposite states.

---

## Meaning Emerges from Construction

Especially with:
- する
- よう
- ている

Meaning is construction-driven rather than isolated-word-driven.

---

## Japanese State Orientation

Japanese frequently encodes:
- resulting state
- maintained state
- transition into state

rather than direct action descriptions.

Examples:

- 死んでいる
- 分かっている
- 結婚している
- 静かにしている
